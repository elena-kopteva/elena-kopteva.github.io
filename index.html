About

Born in Ukraine and raised along the northern coast of Lake Baikal in Siberia, I worked in gravity theory and theoretical cosmology in Ukraine and the Czech Republic before joining the University of Illinois Urbana–Champaign as a Visiting Teaching and Research Faculty member. My work focuses on exact solutions in general relativity: developing models for black holes embedded in cosmological media, studying the interplay between cosmological expansion and local gravity, and investigating perturbations of cosmological black holes and superradiance.

Research

Inhomogeneous cosmological models

Accelerated expansion from non‑standard fluid components and anisotropies.

We study global inhomogeneity via Stephani-class cosmologies (shear-free, non-rotating perfect fluids with spatially varying pressure) where late-time acceleration can arise without a cosmological constant. We derived a closed-form redshift–magnitude relation enabling full fits; our scope includes evaluating observational viability through structure-growth and distance probes (Jeans/Boltzmann perturbations, supernova distance ladder, BAO standard-ruler distances, and structure-growth rates), aiming toward a data-tied viability map for inhomogeneous acceleration.

Black holes in evolving backgrounds

Exact/approximate solutions for compact objects in non‑static cosmological media.

We build and analyze exact GR solutions for black holes embedded in (a) static, radially varying anisotropic media (e.g., dark-energy–like fluids and networks of topological defects) and (b) time-dependent cosmological backgrounds modeled by generalized LTB spacetimes (spherically symmetric, inhomogeneous perfect fluids with nonzero pressure, including dust, radiation, and comoving dust+radiation mixture). Working in a continuum effective-medium limit, we obtain closed-form horizon maps and baseline solutions against which clumpy substructure can be added as perturbations or tested via N-body.
